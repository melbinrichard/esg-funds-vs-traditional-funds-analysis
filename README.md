# ESG Funds vs Traditional Funds Analysis

## Overview

This project compares ESG funds and non-ESG funds to understand whether sustainability-focused investing can also deliver competitive financial performance.

Using fund-level data and Python-based analysis, the project evaluates differences between ESG and non-ESG funds across return performance, risk and volatility, and sustainability-related metrics. Tableau was used to create visual dashboards to support interpretation of the results.

## Objectives

### 1. Do ESG funds perform as well as or better than non-ESG funds financially?
This objective compares ESG and non-ESG fund returns across different time periods to assess whether ESG investing delivers competitive financial returns.

### 2. Do ESG funds demonstrate lower risk and volatility over time?
This objective analyzes return stability, variation, and medium-term performance to understand whether ESG funds offer better risk-adjusted investment behaviour.

### 3. How well do fund holdings align with ESG values?
This objective evaluates ESG and non-ESG funds based on fossil fuel exposure, carbon footprint, carbon intensity, financed emissions, gender equality, and deforestation-related indicators.

## Key Findings

- ESG funds showed stronger performance in the medium term, especially over 3- and 5-year periods.
- ESG funds demonstrated lower relative risk in some return periods.
- ESG funds generally had lower fossil fuel exposure and lower carbon intensity than non-ESG funds.
- However, ESG funds were not always fully aligned with sustainability goals, showing that ESG labels do not automatically guarantee strong environmental performance.
